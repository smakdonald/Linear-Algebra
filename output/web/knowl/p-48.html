Given two vectors spaces \(V\) and \(W\) (over \(\R\)) we say that a function \(T:V\to W\) is a linear map if for all \(\v,\v'\in V\) and \(\l\in \R\) we have
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} T(\v+\v') &= T(\v) + T(\v') \\ T(\l\,\v) &= \l\, T(\v). \qedhere \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
