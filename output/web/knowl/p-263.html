Let's consider the counterclockwise rotation of angle \(\theta\) in the plane. Denote this by \(R_\theta:\R^2 \to \R^2\text{.}\) Then \(R_\theta(1,0)=(\cos \theta, \sin \theta)\) and \(R_\theta(0,1)=(-\sin \theta, \cos \theta)\text{,}\) thus the matrix of \(R_\theta\) with respect to the standard basis is
\begin{equation*} [R_\theta]= \begin{bmatrix} \cos \theta & -\sin \theta\\ \sin \theta & \cos \theta \end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}
