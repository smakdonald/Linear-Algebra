Example 1.17.

Given a line \(L\text{,}\) the map \(P:\R^2\to \R^2\) given by \(P(u)=\) the projection of \(u\) onto the direction of \(L\) is a linear map. According to this map is additive, that is, \(P(u+v)=P(u)+P(v)\) and it is not hard to see that \(P(\lambda u)=\lambda P(u)\) also holds.
