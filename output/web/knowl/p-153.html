The effect of doing the step \((3,7)\) and then the step \((2,-3)\) is identical with the effect of doing the step \((5,4)\text{.}\) Moreover, this operation feels a lot like adding the steps together. Similarly, adding \((2,-3)\) to itself gives us a step that we very naturally think of as twice \((2,-3)\text{,}\) or \(2 \,(2,-3) = (4,-6)\text{.}\) It seems natural to think of this operation as that of multiplying the step \((2,-3)\) by the number \(2\text{.}\) (See [cross-reference to target(s) "fig:vecalg" missing or not unique])
