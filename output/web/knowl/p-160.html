Let's think about one of the most basic geometric objects there is: a line in the plane. There are two natural ways to specify a line. The description that probably first comes to mind is that of the solution set of a linear equation. The line \(x+2y = 6\) has slope \(-1/2\) and goes through \((6,0)\text{;}\) the line \(0x+y = 7\) is horizontal and goes through \((0,7)\text{;}\) and the line \(x+0y = 3\) is vertical, going through \((3,0)\text{.}\) (See [cross-reference to target(s) "fig:lines" missing or not unique].)
