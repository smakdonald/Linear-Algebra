If \(S\of V\) is linearly independent then for all \(\v\in \linspan(S)\) there is a unique way of writing \(\v\) as a linear combination from \(S\) in the following sense: for all \(\v\in \linspan(S)\) there is a unique finite subset \(\set{\vs} \of S\) and unique corresponding coefficients \(\ls\in \F\setminus\{0\}\) such that \(\Slv = \v\text{.}\)
