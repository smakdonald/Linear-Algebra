Consider the subspace \(V =\linspan\set{\sin(kx),\cos(kx)\mid 0\leq k\leq n} \of C[0,2\pi]\text{.}\) Let the linear map \(D:V\to V\) be the differentiation map \(D(f) = f'\text{.}\) I claim that \(D^*:V\to V\) is given by
\begin{equation*} D^* = -D. \end{equation*}
To prove this we have to show that for all \(f\in F_n\) and \(g\in F_n\) we have \(\ip{D(f),g} = \ip{f,-D(g)}\text{.}\) This is simply integration by parts:
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \ip{D(f),g} &= \definite f'(x) g(x) dx0{2\pi} \\ &= \left. f(x) g(x) \vphantom{\Big(}\right|_0^{2\pi} - \definite f(x) g'(x) dx0{2\pi} \\ &= -\ip{f,D(g)} = \ip{f,-D(g)}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
