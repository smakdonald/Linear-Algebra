To prove that \(\ker(T)+\range(T^*)\) is all of \(V\) we're going to give a dimension argument and use that \(\rank(T) = \rank(T^*)\text{,}\) or in other words that \(\dim(\range(T)) = \dim(\range(T^*))\text{.}\) We'll start by proving that \(\rank(T^*)\le \rank(T)\text{.}\) Consider then the linear map
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \tilde{T}\colon \range(T^*) &\to \range(T) \\ \v &\mapsto T(\v) \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
This map has trivial kernel, exactly by the argument above: a vector \(\v\) that was in \(\range(T^*)\) and had \(T(\v)=\0\) would be in \(\ker(T)\cap \range(T^*)=\set{\0}\text{.}\) By [cross-reference to target(s) "thm:dim_monotone" missing or not unique] we get \(\dim(\range(T^*))\le \dim\range(T)\text{.}\) Now using [cross-reference to target(s) "lem:transtrans" missing or not unique] again we have \(\rank(T)=\rank(T^{**} )\le \rank(T^*)\text{.}\) This tells us that \(\ker(T) \odsum \range(T^*)\) is a subspace of \(V\) having dimension \(\dim(V)\text{,}\) so the two spaces are equal.
