We first verify that \(f\) satisfies \(f(x_i) = y_i\) for all \(1\leq i\leq n\text{.}\) This follows from [cross-reference to target(s) "eq:lagrange" missing or not unique]:
\begin{equation*} f(x_i)=y_1\ell_1(x_i)+y_2\ell_2(x_i)+ \cdots+ y_i\ell_i(x_i)+\cdots +y_n\ell_n(x_i)=y_1\cdot 0+\cdots+y_i\cdot 1+\cdots+y_n\cdot 0 =y_i. \end{equation*}
Now consider the evaluation map \(\text{ev}:V_{n-1}\to \F^n\) given by \(\text{ev}(p(x))=(p(x_1),p(x_2),\ldots, p(x_n))\text{.}\) We have just shown that this map is surjective and we know \(\dim V_{n-1}=n=\dim \F^n\text{.}\) According to [cross-reference to target(s) "cor:injsurjbij" missing or not unique] we deduce that \(\text{ev}\) is bijective, so the uniqueness of \(f\) follows.
