As we mentioned at the start, linear algebra connects algebra and geometry, so we'd like to make a connection between these two descriptions. Let's start by thinking about the vertical line through \((3,0)\text{.}\) This is all the points whose \(x\) coordinate is \(3\text{,}\) or, in other words, all the points whose distance in the direction of the \(x\)-axis is \(3\text{.}\) Similarly the horizontal line through \((0,7)\) consists of all the points in the plane whose vertical distance from the origin is \(7\text{.}\) Let's try to generalize this notion of “distance along an axis”.
