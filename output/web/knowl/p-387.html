For a) suppose that \(B_1\) and \(B_2\) are bases of \(V\text{.}\) By symmetry it suffices to prove that \(\abs{B_1}\le \abs{B_2}\text{.}\) Since \(B_1\) is (in particular) linearly independent, and \(B_2\) is spanning, the Steinitz exchange lemma tells us that there exists a spanning set \(T\) with \(B_1\of T\) and \(\abs{T}\le \abs{B_2}\text{.}\) We thus have \(\abs{B_1}\le \abs{T} \le \abs{B_2}\text{.}\)
