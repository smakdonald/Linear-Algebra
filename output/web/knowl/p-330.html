  1. \(D:\R[X]\to \R[X]\) where \(D(p)=p'\) is the derivative map is NOT injective. Indeed the constant polynomials \(p(x)=5\) and \(q(x)=7\) satisfy \(D(p)=D(q)=0\) but of course \(p\neq q\text{.}\)
  2. the map \(C:\R[X]\to \R^\N\) which takes a polynomial \(p(X)=a_0+a_1X+a_2X^2+\cdots a_dX^d\) to its sequence of coefficients \(C(p)=(a_0,a_1,a_2,\ldots, a_d, 0, 0,0, \ldots)\) is injective.
