If \(T:C[0,1]\to \R\) is given by \(T(f)=\int_{0}^1 f(x)dx\) then the range of \(T\) is \(\range(T) =\R\text{.}\) To see this take any \(r\in\R\) and consider the constant function \(f_r:[0,1]\to\R, f_r(x)=r\text{.}\) Then \(f_r\in C[0,1]\) and \(T(f_r)=\int_{0}^1 f_r(x) dx=\int_{0}^1 r dx= r\text{.}\) In conclusion, every \(r\in \R\) is in \(\range(T)\text{.}\)
