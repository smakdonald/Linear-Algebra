If \(B\) is a symmetric bilinear form on a finite dimensional vector space \(V\) over a field \(\F\) in which \(2\not=0\) then \(V\) has a basis \(D=\{\v_1,\ldots, \v_n\}\) with respect to which the matrix \([B]_D\) is diagonal. Moreover there exist scalars \(\l_1,\ldots, \l_n\in \F\) so that the quadratic form defined by \(B\) satisfies
\begin{equation*} Q(x_1\v_1+\cdots+x_n\v_n)=\l_1x_1^2+\cdots+\l_nx_n^2. \end{equation*}
