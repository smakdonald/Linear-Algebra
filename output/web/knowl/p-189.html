If \(V\) is a vector space over \(\F\) and \(S\of V\) then \(S\) is linearly independent if every linear combination of (distinct) vectors from \(S\) equaling \(\0\) has all its coefficients equal to \(0\text{.}\) In other words for all \(\vs\in S\text{,}\) \(\ls\in \F\) with \(\v_i\not=\v_j\) for \(i\not= j\) we have
\begin{equation*} \Slv = \0 \quad\implies\quad \l_1=\l_2=\dots=\l_n=0. \end{equation*}
