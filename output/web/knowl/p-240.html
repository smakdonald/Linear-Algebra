Finally, let \(I\of V\) be linearly independent and let \(S\) be a finite spanning set for \(V\text{.}\) (Such an \(S\) exists by the definition of finite dimensionality.) By part b) there is a basis \(B\) contained in \(S\text{.}\) By the Steinitz exchange property there is a spanning set \(T\) with \(I\of T\) and \(\abs{T}\le \abs{B}\text{.}\) By b) we know that \(T\) contains a basis, but unless that basis is exactly \(T\) then we would have found a basis for \(V\) strictly smaller than \(B\text{,}\) contradicting a). Thus \(T\) is a basis containing \(I\text{.}\)
