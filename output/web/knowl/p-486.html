shows that if \(U\) is a subspace of a finite dimensional inner product space \(V\text{,}\) then for each \(\v\in V\) there exist unique vectors \(\u \in U\) and \(\w\in U\perp\) (which depend on \(\v\)) so that \(\v=\u+\w\text{.}\) Then the projection of \(\v\) onto \(U\) is \({\proj}_{U}(\v)=\u\) and the projection map from \(V\) to \(U\) is the function \(P:V\to U, P(\v)= {\proj}_{U}(\v)=\u\text{.}\)
