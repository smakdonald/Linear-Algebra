Let \(x_1,\ldots,x_n\in \F\) be distinct and let \(y_1,\ldots,y_n\in \F\text{.}\) There is a unique polynomial \(f\in \F[x]\) of degree at most \(n-1\) such that \(f(x_i) = y_i\) for all \(1\leq i\leq n\text{,}\) written in terms of the Lagrange basis as
\begin{equation*} f(x)=y_1\ell_1+y_2\ell_2+\cdots +y_n\ell_n. \end{equation*}
