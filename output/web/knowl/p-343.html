\(\set{1,\sqrt 2} \of \R\) is linearly independent when we think of \(\R\) as a vector space over \(\Q\text{.}\) Suppose \(\l,\mu\in \Q\) satisfy \(\l 1 + \mu \sqrt 2=0\text{.}\) If \(\mu=0\) we have \(\l 1 = \l=0\text{.}\) On the other hand if \(\mu\not=0\) then \(\sqrt2 = -\l/\mu \in \Q\) which we know is impossible.
