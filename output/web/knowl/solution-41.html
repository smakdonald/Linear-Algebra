Solution 1.4.1.

If \(x,y\) satisfy \(x+2y = 6\) we can write \(x=-2y+6\) and so
\begin{equation*} (x,y)=(-2y+6,y)=(6,0)+y(-2,1) \end{equation*}
tells us the point \((x,y)\) can be reached by taking the step \((6,0)\) after which we move in the direction of the step \((2,-1)\) either forward of backward \(|y|\) times the length of \((2,-1)\text{.}\)
