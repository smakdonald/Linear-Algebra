If we let \(V = \R[X]\) and \(W=\setof{p . (x^2+1)}{p \in \R[X]}\) then the quotient \(V/W\) is the vector space of real polynomials, except that we have erased the difference between \(X^2+1\) and \(0\text{,}\) or to put it another way, between \(X^2\) and \(-1\text{.}\) Thus, for instance,
\begin{equation*} X^2 + W = X^2 -1(X^2+1) + W = 1+W , \end{equation*}
so \(X^2=1\) in this space. We have just constructed the complex numbers!
