Consider the matrix
\begin{equation*} A = \begin{bmatrix} 1&2&-6\\2&9&8\\-6&8&-1 \end{bmatrix} \in M_{3\by 3}(\R). \end{equation*}
As in we may construct a bilinear form
\begin{equation*} B_A:\R^3\times \R^3\to \R, \quad B_A(\u,\v)=\u^TA\v. \end{equation*}
which determines a quadratic function by the formula \(Q(\v)=B(\v,\v)=\v^tA\v\) for all \(\v\in\R^3\text{.}\) The matrix of \(B\) with respect to the standard basis \(D=\{\e_1,\e_2,\e_3\}\) is \([B]_D=A\text{.}\) Let \(\v=(x,y,z)\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} Q(x,y,z)=\begin{bmatrix} x & y& z \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1&2&-6\\2&9&8\\-6&8&-1 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix}=x^2 + 9y^2 - z^2 + 4xy - 12xz + 16yz. \end{equation*}
