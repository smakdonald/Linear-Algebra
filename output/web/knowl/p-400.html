The system
\begin{equation*} \begin{cases} \systeme{ x_1-2x_3=0,x_1+x_2+x_4=0} \end{cases} \end{equation*}
is equivalent to the following system in reduced row echelon form
\begin{equation*} \begin{cases} \systeme{ x_1-2x_3=0,x_2+2x_3+x_4=0} \end{cases} \end{equation*}
in which the variables \(x_1, x_2\) are dependent (correspond to pivots) and the variables \(x_3, x_4\) are free. The general solution to this system is
\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}2x_3 \\ -2x_3-x_4 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix}=x_3\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} +x_4\begin{bmatrix}0 \\ -1 \\ 0\\ 1 \end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}
By the work above the vectors \((2,-2,1,0), (0,-1,0,1)\) span \(V\) and are linearly independent so they form a basis for \(V\text{.}\) In particular, \(\dim(V)=2\text{.}\)
