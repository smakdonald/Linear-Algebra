For the second claim, first note that one inclusion, \(\linspan(S)\of \linspan(S\cup \{\v\})\text{,}\) always holds, by part (a) since \(S\of S\cup\{\v\}\text{.}\) Now that \(\w\in \linspan(S\cup \{\v\})\text{.}\) By definition of span, there exist \(v_1,\ldots, v_n\in S\) and \(\l_1,\ldots, \l_n,\mu \in \F\) so that
\begin{equation*} \w=\sum_{i=1}^n \l_i\v_i+\mu\v. \end{equation*}
Since \(\v\in \linspan(S)\) we also have \(v'_1,\ldots, v'_m\in S\) and \(\gamma_1,\ldots, \gamma_m \in \F\) so that
\begin{equation*} \v=\sum_{j=1}^m \gamma_j\v'_j. \end{equation*}
Plugging in to the equation above gives \(\w=\sum_{i=1}^n \l_i\v_i+\sum_{j=1}^m \gamma_j\v'_j\text{,}\) that is, \(\w\in \linspan(S)\text{.}\) We have shown that \(\linspan(S\cup \{\v\})\of \linspan(S)\) which completes the proof that \(\linspan(S\cup \{\v\})= \linspan(S)\text{.}\)
