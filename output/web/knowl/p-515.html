It is definitely possible to give a rather long but very direct proof of this, but to illustrate the power of the Rank-Nullity Theorem ([cross-reference to target(s) "thm:ranknullity" missing or not unique]) we'll give a shorter proof. Consider the (linear) map
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \pi \colon V &\to V/W \\ \v &\mapsto \v + W. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
Note that \(\pi\) is clearly surjective since for any \(\v+W\in V/W\) we have \(\pi(\v) = \v+W\text{.}\) Similarly the kernel of \(\pi\) is the set of \(\v\in V\) such that \(\v+W = W\text{.}\) This happens exactly when \(\v\in W-W=W\text{,}\) so \(\ker(\pi)=W\text{.}\) Thus [cross-reference to target(s) "thm:ranknullity" missing or not unique] gives us
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \dim(V) &= \dim(V/W) + \dim(W), \\ \shortintertext{i.e.,} \dim(V/W) &= \dim(V) - \dim(W). \qedhere \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
