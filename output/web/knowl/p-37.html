A polynomial with real coefficients is an expression of the form \(p(X)=a_dX^d+ a_{d-1}X^{d-1}+ \cdots +a_1X+a_0\text{,}\) where \(a_d, \ldots, a_0\in\R\text{.}\) The set of all polynomials with real coefficients is denoted
\begin{equation*} \R[X]=\{a_dX^d+ a_{d-1}X^{d-1}+ \cdots +a_1X+a_0 \mid a_0, \ldots, a_d\in \R\}. \end{equation*}
a vector space. We already know how to add polynomials and multiply them by real numbers. If \(p\) and \(q\) are polynomials with real coefficients and \(\l\in \R\) then we have new polynomials \(p+q\) and \(\l\, p\) with
\begin{equation*} (p+q)(X) = p(X) + q(X) \quad\text{and}\quad (\l p)(X) = \l\, p(X). \end{equation*}
