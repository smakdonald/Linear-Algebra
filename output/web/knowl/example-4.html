Example 1.10.

We can go even further and think about sequences of infinite length, such as those that appear in calculus and analysis.
\begin{equation*} \R^\N = \setof{(a_n)_{n\ge 0}}{\text{$\forall n\in \N$ we have $a_n\in \R$}} = \setof{a}{\text{$a$ is a function $\N\to \R$}}. \end{equation*}
We can add and scale such things in the natural way:
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} (a_n)_{n\ge 0} + (b_n)_{n\ge 0} &= (a_n+b_n)_{n\ge 0} \\ \l\, (a_n)_{n\ge 0} &= (\l a_n)_{n\ge 0}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
