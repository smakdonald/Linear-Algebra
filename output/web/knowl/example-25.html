Example 2.32.

The set of constant polynomials is a subspace of \(\R[X]\) as it is the kernel of the derivative map \(D:\R[X]\to \R[X]\) is given by \(D(p)=\frac{d}{dX}(p)\text{.}\)
We now see that we can use the notion of kernel and image to analyze properties of linear maps such as being injective or being surjective.
