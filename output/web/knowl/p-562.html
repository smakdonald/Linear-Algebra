Applying linear maps to vectors (evaluating linear maps) is a bilinear function:
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} e \colon L(V,W)\times V &\to W \\ (T,\v) &\mapsto T(\v) \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
is bilinear since \(T(\l\u+\mu\v)=\l T(\u)+\mu T(\v)\) and also \((\l T+\mu S)(\v) = \l T(\v)+\mu S(\v)\text{.}\)
