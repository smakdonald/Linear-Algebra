Theorem 6.1.

If \(V,W\) are finite dimensional and \(T:V\to W\) is linear then there are bases \(B,B'\) for \(V,W\) respectively such that the matrix of \(T\) has the form
\begin{equation*} [T]_B^{B'}=\mat[1\\&1\\&&\ddots\\&&&1&\phantom{0}&\phantom{0}&\phantom{0}\\\\\\] \end{equation*}
where all entries are \(0\) except for \(r\) \(1\)s on the main diagonal, where
\begin{equation*} r=\rank(T)=\dim(\range(T)). \end{equation*}

Proof.
