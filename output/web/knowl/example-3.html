Example 1.9.

Similarly we can talk about vectors with more then two entries: \(\R^3\) for instance, but also \(\R^4\text{,}\) \(\R^5\text{,}\) â€¦, etc. Formally we have
\begin{equation*} \R^n = \setof{(x_1, x_2, \dots, x_{n})}{x_1, x_2, \dots, x_{n} \in \R}, \end{equation*}
and we define
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} (x_1, x_2, \dots, x_{n}) + (y_1, y_2, \dots, y_{n}) &= (x_1+y_1,x_2+y_2,\dots,x_n+y_n) \\ \l\, (x_1, x_2, \dots, x_{n}) &= (\l\, x_1, \l\, x_2, \dots, \l\, x_{n}), \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
where \(\l\) is a real number.
