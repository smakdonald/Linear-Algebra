We know that the evaluation function \(e_{3/2} : V_{10}\to \R\) defined by \(e_{3/2}(p)=p(3/2)\) is a linear map from \(V_n\) to \(\R\text{.}\) The Riesz Representation Theorem tells us that there is some fixed polynomial \(q\) such that for all \(p\in V_{10}\) we have
\begin{equation*} p\left(\frac32\right) = e_{3/2}(p) = \ip{p,q} = \definite p(x) q(x) dx{-1}1. \end{equation*}
It turns out that this polynomial is
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} q(x) = \frac{736696275275961}{134217728} &\, x^{10} + \frac{17640939135105}{16777216} \, x^{9} -\frac{1717751356101495}{134217728} \, x^{8} \\ &- \frac{9177061112505}{4194304} \, x^{7} +\frac{693697040221185}{67108864} \, x^{6} \\ &+ \frac{12562845410115}{8388608} \, x^{5} -\frac{225576769693275}{67108864} \, x^{4} \\ &-\frac{1563597220185}{4194304} \, x^{3} +\frac{50390971636005}{134217728} \, x^{2} \\ &+\frac{409434133185}{16777216} \, x - \frac{876496780467}{134217728}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
