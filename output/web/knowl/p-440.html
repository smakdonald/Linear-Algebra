Rrecall that \(T(\e_1), T(\e_2), T(\e_3), T(\e_4)\) are the columns of the matrix of the linear transformation \(T\)
\begin{equation*} A= \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -2 & 0\\ 1 & 1 & 0 & 1\end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}
We want to find a basis for the vector space spanned by the columns of \(A\text{.}\) We can do so by using column ofperations to put \(A\) in column echelon form
\begin{equation*} A \xrightarrow{CEF} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 1 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}
We thus see that \(\range(T)\) has basis \(\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\0\end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\1\end{bmatrix}\right\}\text{.}\) Thus \(\dim \range(T)=2\text{.}\)
