The proof is straightforward definition checking. As a representative sample I'll prove that vector addition is associative. For \(R,S,T \in L(V,W)\) and \(\v\in V\) we have
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \parens[\big]{R+(S+T)}(\v) &= R(\v) + (S+T)(\v) \\ &= R(\v) + (S(\v)+T(\v)) \\ &= (R(\v)+S(\v))+T(\v) \\ &= (R+S)(\v)+T(\v) \\ &= \parens[\big]{(R+S)+T}(\v), \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
where the central equality is associativity of vector addition in \(W\) and everything else is the definition of addition in \(L(V,W)\text{.}\)
