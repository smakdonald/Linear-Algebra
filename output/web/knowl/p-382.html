Let \(\v=(v_1, \ldots, v_d)\) be a vector in \(\R^d\text{.}\) Then the Euclidean norm of \(\v\) is
\begin{equation*} \|\v\|=\sqrt{v_1^2+v_2^2+\cdots +c_d^2} \end{equation*}
The Euclidean distance between points \(\x\) and \(\boldsymbol{\mu}\) in \(\R^d\) is
\begin{equation*} \|\mathbf{x} - \boldsymbol{\mu}\|=\sqrt{(x_1-\mu_1)^2+(x_2-\mu_2)^2+\cdots +(x_d-\mu_d)^2}. \end{equation*}
