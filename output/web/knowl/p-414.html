Exaluating non-linear maps though, for example polynomials is not a bilinear map. Specifically the map \((p,x)\mapsto p(x)\) from \(\R[X]\times \R\) to \(\R\) is not bilinear. It is linear in \(p\text{,}\) but not in \(x\) since, for example, if \(p=X^2\) then \(4=p(1+1) \not= p(1)+p(1)=2\text{.}\)
