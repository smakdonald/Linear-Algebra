If \(\v\in V\) then the vector \(T^{**}\) is the unique vector in \(W\) satisfying
\begin{equation*} \ip{T^*(\w),\v} = \ip{\w,T^{**} (\v)} \end{equation*}
for all \(\w\in W\text{.}\) We'll show that \(T(\v)\) has this property; for all \(\w\in W\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \ip{T^*(\w),\v} &=\overline{ \ip{\v,T^*(\w)}} \\ &=\overline{ \ip{T(\v),\w}} \\ &= \overline{ \overline{\ip{\w,T(\v)}}}=\ip{\w,T(\v)}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
Thus \(T^{**} (\v)=T(\v)\) for all \(\v\in V\text{.}\)
