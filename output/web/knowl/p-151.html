One of the first examples of vectors people encountered were displacement vectors, or as we'll call them, steps. A step in the plane is the process of moving a certain distance in a certain direction. For instance \((2,-1)\) is the action of moving \(2\) units right and \(1\) unit down. So, using a bit of trigonometry, we see that the step moves you a distance \(\sqrt{2^2+(-1)^2}\) in the direction \(\theta = \arctan(-1/2)\) in polar coordinates.
