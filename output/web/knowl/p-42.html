Let's take our first example of adding vectors and apply a rotation to each one. In other words let's define a map \(R:\R^2\to \R^2\) that rotates any step by (in this case) \(110^\circ\text{.}\) The diagram demonstrating that \((3,7)+(2,-3)=(5,4)\) rotates together to give a demonstration that
\begin{equation*} R(3,7) + R(2,-3) = R(5,4) = R\parens[\big]{(3,7)+(2,-3)}. \end{equation*}
