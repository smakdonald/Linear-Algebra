If we make things a little more symmetric, as mathematicians are prone to do, everything becomes much nicer, and a calculation that was, all along, geometry, geometry, geometry, suddenly becomes algebra. Let's remind ourselves how. If we write \(\abs{\u}\) for the length of \(\u\text{,}\) recall that the distance of \(\u\) along the axis is \(\abs{\u}\cos(\theta)\text{.}\)
