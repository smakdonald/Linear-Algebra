If \(U,V,W\) are vector spaces over a field \(\F\) then \(B:U\times V\to W\) is bilinear if it is linear in each argument. That is to say for all \(\u,\u'\in U\text{,}\) \(\v,\v'\in V\) and \(\l,\mu\in \F\) we have
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} B(\lambda \u+\mu \u',\v) &= \lambda \, B(\u,\v)+\mu \, B(\u',\v) \\ B(\u,\lambda \v+\mu \v') &= \lambda \, B(\u,\v)+\mu \, B(\u,\v') \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
