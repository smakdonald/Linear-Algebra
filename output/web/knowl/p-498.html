We first need to prove that \(+, \cdot\) are maps to \(V/W\text{,}\) or in other words that \(A+B\) and \(\l B\) are affine subspaces parallel to \(W\text{.}\) To prove affine-ness set \(A=\v+W\) and \(B=\v'+W\) and note that
\begin{equation*} A+B=(\v+W)+(\v'+W)=(\v+\v')+(W+W)=(\v+\v')+W. \end{equation*}
and if \(\l\neq 0\)
\begin{equation*} \l A=\l(\v+W)=\l\v+\l W=\l\v +W. \end{equation*}
and also if \(\l=0\)
\begin{equation*} 0 A= W=\0+W=0\v +W. \end{equation*}
To show that \(V/W\) is a vector space we would now have to check the axioms of a vector space. I will not do this explicitly.
