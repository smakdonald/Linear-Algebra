If \(B:V\times V\to\F\) is a bilinear form and \(D=\set{\v_1,\v_2,\dots,\v_n}\) is an ordered basis for \(V\) then the matrix for \(B\) with respect to \(D\) is the \(n\by n\) matrix \([B]_D\) with entries
\begin{equation*} ([B]_D)_{ij} = B(\v_i,\v_j). \end{equation*}
We have
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} B\left(\sum_1^n \lambda_i \v_i,\sum_1^n \mu_j \v_j\right)&=\sum_{i=1}^n \sum_{j=1}^n \lambda_i\mu_j B(\v_i,\v_j)\\ &=\sum_{i,j=1}^n \lambda_i \,([B]_D)_{ij}\, \mu_j\\ &=\row{\l_1 & \l_2 & \cdots & \l_n}\, [B]_D \begin{bmatrix} \mu_1\\ \mu_2\\ \vdots \\ \mu_n \end{bmatrix} . \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
