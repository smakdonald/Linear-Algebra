To summarize, if we consider the coefficient matrix of the system
\begin{equation*} A = \cmat[1&2&-3\\ 1&1&-2 \\ 2&7&-2]. \end{equation*}
the perspective of planes consider the dot products of \((x,y,z)\) with the rows of \(A\text{,}\) while the perspective of linear combinations considers linear combinations of the columns of \(A\) with undetermined coefficients. We will see throughout that being able to see such things from alternative viewpoints allows us a much more interesting view of the world of linear algebra around us.
