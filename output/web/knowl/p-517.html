Suppose that \(U\) is a subspace of an inner product space \(V\) and we consider the inclusion map
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} T \colon U &\to V \\ \u &\mapsto \u. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
What is \(T^*\text{?}\) It is the orthogonal projection onto \(U\text{.}\) Since \(V = U\odsum U\perp\) we can represent any vector \(\v\in V\) uniquely as \(\v=\u+\w\) where \(\u\in U\) and \(\w\in U\perp\text{.}\) The projection map is the map \(P:V\to U\) sending \(\v\) to \(\u\text{.}\) To prove that \(T^* = P\) consider \(\u'\in U\) and \(\v\in V\) with \(\v=\u+\w\) as before. Thus \(T(\u')=\u'\) and \(P(\v)=\u\text{.}\) We need to show that \(\ip{T(\u'),\v} = \ip{\u',P(\v)}\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \ip{T(\u'),\v} = \ip{\u',\u+\w} = \ip{\u',\u} + \ip{\u',\w} = \ip{\u',\u} = \ip{\u',P(\v)}, \end{equation*}
as required.
