The final piece of the puzzle is the fact that by [cross-reference to target(s) "thm:four_subspaces" missing or not unique] we know exactly what \(\range(T)\perp\) is: it's just \(\ker(T^*)\) and over the real numbers \(T^*(\w)=M^T\w\text{.}\) Thus we can compute as follows using that \(\vv{y}=\vv{p}+\vv{n}\) with \(\vv{n}\in \range(T)\perp\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} M\begin{bmatrix} A\\B \end{bmatrix} &= \vv{p}\\ &= \vv{y}-\vv{n}\\ M\trans M \begin{bmatrix} A\\B \end{bmatrix}&= M\trans (\vv{y}-\vv{n}) \\ &= M\trans\vv{y}. \intertext{By the previous lemma $M^TM$ is invertible so there is a unique solution} \begin{bmatrix} A\\B \end{bmatrix} &= (M\trans M)\inv M\trans \vv{y}\\ \vv{p}= M\begin{bmatrix} A\\B \end{bmatrix} &= M (M\trans M)\inv M\trans \vv{y}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
