Let's find a basis for the following subspace of \(\R^4\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} V=\left \{(x_1,x_2,x_3,x_4)\mid \begin{cases}\systeme{ x_1-2x_3=0,x_1+x_2+x_4=0}\end{cases}\right\}. \end{equation*}
We know that \(V\) is a subspace since it is the kernel of the linear map
\begin{equation*} T:\R^4\to \R^2, T(x_1,x_2,x_3,x_4)=(x_1-2x_3,x_1+x_2+x_4)=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -2 & 0\\ 1 & 1 & 0 & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \\ x_4 \end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}
By [cross-reference to target(s) "lem:kerrangesubspaces" missing or not unique] the kernels (nullspaces) are subspaces.
