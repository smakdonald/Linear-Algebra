The multiplication function \(m:\R^2\to \R, m((x,y))=xy\) is not linear since, for instance, \(m((2,3)+(1,5)) = 24 \not= m(2,3)+m(1,5)\text{.}\) However the definition of a field requires that \(m\) be linear in each argument:
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} m(\lambda x+\mu x',y)&=\lambda \,m(x,y)+\mu \, m(x',y),\\ m(x,\lambda y+\mu y')&=\lambda \,m(x,y)+\mu \, m(x,y'). \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
