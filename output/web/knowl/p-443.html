Let \(\set{\us[k]}\) be a basis for \(\ker(T)\of V\) and \(\set{\ws[\ell]}\) a basis for \(\range(T)\of W\text{.}\) By definition there exist vectors \(\set{\vs[\ell]}\) such that for each \(i\) we have \(T(\v_i)=\w_i\text{.}\) We claim that \(B = \set{\us[k],\vs[\ell]}\) is a basis for \(V\text{,}\) and hence that \(V\) has dimension \(k+\ell = \dim(\ker(T))+\dim(\range(T))\text{.}\) Let's show first that \(B\) is linearly independent. To that end suppose that \(\ls[k]\) and \(\mus[\ell]\) in \(\F\) are such that
\begin{equation*} \sum_1^k \l_i \u_i + \sum_1^\ell \mu_j \v_j = \0. \end{equation*}
Applying \(T\) we get \(\sum_1^\ell \mu_j \w_j = \0\) (since each \(\u_i\) is in the kernel of \(T\)) so by the linear independence of the \(\w_j\) we have \(\mu_1=\mu_2=\dots=\mu_\ell = 0\text{.}\) This tells us in turn that \(\sum_1^k \l_i \u_i = \0\text{,}\) forcing \(\l_1=\l_2=\dots=\l_k=0\text{.}\)
